# Impact-of-Crude-Oil-Prices-on-Indian-Economy

## Overview

This project analyzes the impact of crude oil price fluctuations on major macroeconomic indicators in India using monthly data from 2012–2025. The study applies statistical and econometric techniques to understand both short-run and long-run relationships between crude oil prices and key economic variables.

## Techniques Used

* Pearson Correlation Analysis
* Spearman Correlation Analysis
* Partial Correlation Analysis
* Linear Regression
* Multivariate Regression
* Principal Component Analysis (PCA)
* Factor Analysis
* Structural Vector Autoregression (SVAR)
* SARIMAX-GARCH
* Vector Error Correction Model (VECM)

## Tools Used

* R
* SPSS
* Excel

## Key Findings

* Oil prices showed a strong positive relationship with Inflation and Wholesale Price Index (WPI).
* WPI emerged as a major transmission channel through which oil price fluctuations affect the economy.
* Oil price shocks significantly influenced inflation, monetary policy, and industrial activity.
* Exchange rate and inflation dynamics were closely associated with crude oil price movements.

## Conclusion

The study concludes that crude oil prices are an important external determinant of India's macroeconomic performance. The strongest impact was observed on inflation and wholesale prices, while consumer prices and industrial output were affected more indirectly through broader economic channels. Advanced econometric techniques such as SVAR, SARIMAX-GARCH, and VECM confirmed both short-run and long-run effects of oil price fluctuations on key economic indicators. The findings highlight the importance of effective energy, monetary, and fiscal policies to reduce vulnerability to global oil price shocks.
